Food of Maharashtra

Bhakri

Bhakri is a traditional flatbread made from jowar (sorghum), bajra (millet), or rice flour. It's dense, gluten-free, and often served hot with chutney, thecha (spicy green chili paste), or vegetable curries. Bhakri is a staple in rural Maharashtra and symbolizes rustic simplicity and hearty nutrition.

Varan Bhaat

Varan Bhaat is a comfort food of Maharashtra, consisting of plain toor dal (yellow pigeon peas) seasoned lightly with turmeric and ghee, served over steamed rice. It's often accompanied by lemon, pickle, or papad. This simple dish is also an essential part of Maharashtrian festive and wedding meals.

Misal Pav

Misal Pav is a spicy, tangy, and flavorful dish made with sprouted moth beans (matki), topped with farsan, onions, lemon, and coriander. Served with pav, it's a popular breakfast or snack. The dish varies across regions, with Kolhapuri misal being the spiciest. It's known for its explosive flavors.

Puran Poli

Puran Poli is a festive flatbread made by stuffing sweetened cooked chana dal (Bengal gram) and jaggery (called "puran") into a soft wheat dough and cooking it on a griddle. It is especially popular during Holi and Ganesh Chaturthi and is often served with ghee or milk.

Vada Pav

Vada Pav is Mumbai's iconic street food, often called the Indian burger. It features a spicy potato fritter (vada) sandwiched between pav (bread roll), along with garlic chutney and green chilies. Affordable, filling, and flavorful, it is a go-to snack for locals and travelers alike.

Malvani Fish Curry

Malvani fish curry is a fiery and flavorful coconut-based curry from the Konkan coast. It uses freshly ground spices, dried kokum, and a special Malvani masala. Typically made with fish like surmai (kingfish) or bangda (mackerel), it's best served with rice or bhakri.

Sabudana Khichdi

Sabudana Khichdi is a fasting dish made with tapioca pearls, peanuts, green chilies, and potatoes. It is a favorite during Hindu fasting days like Ekadashi and Navratri. Despite its fasting label, it's tasty and energizing, offering a good balance of carbs and protein from sago and peanuts.

Kolambi Bhaat

Kolambi Bhaat is a one-pot rice dish made with prawns (kolambi), aromatic spices, and sometimes coconut milk. Popular in coastal Maharashtra, especially the Konkan region, it combines the richness of seafood with fragrant rice, making it a unique and deeply satisfying main course.

Kothimbir Vadi

Kothimbir Vadi is a savory snack made with coriander leaves (kothimbir), chickpea flour (besan), and spices. The batter is steamed, sliced, and then shallow- or deep-fried. Crisp on the outside and soft inside, it’s commonly served with green chutney or ketchup as a tea-time snack.

Shrikhand

Shrikhand is a sweet dish made from hung curd (strained yogurt), flavored with cardamom, saffron, and sugar. Sometimes garnished with nuts and fruits, it's served chilled and often paired with puris. Popular in both Maharashtra and Gujarat, it’s a festive dessert enjoyed during special occasions and summer months.

Modak

Modak is a sweet dumpling with a soft rice flour or wheat shell filled with a mixture of grated coconut and jaggery. Steamed or fried, it’s especially associated with Lord Ganesha during Ganesh Chaturthi. The steamed version is called "ukdiche modak" and is often topped with ghee before serving.

Basundi

Basundi is a rich, creamy dessert made by slow-cooking milk until thickened and flavored with cardamom, saffron, and sugar. Often garnished with chopped nuts, it’s served chilled and is similar to North India’s rabri. It’s a popular sweet during festivals, weddings, and special celebrations.

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a rich, buttery mash of vegetables like potatoes, tomatoes, and peas cooked with special spices, served with buttered pav (bread rolls). Originating from Mumbai, it started as a quick lunch for mill workers and has since become a beloved street food across India.
