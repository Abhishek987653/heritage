Art & Craft of Maharashtra

Warli Painting


Origin: Warli tribe of northern Maharashtra (Palghar district)

Style: Simple, monochromatic (white on mud background), using geometric shapes like circles, triangles, and squares

Themes: Nature, daily life, farming, animals, and tribal rituals

Chitrakathi Painting


Origin: Pinguli village near Sawantwadi

Medium: Narrative paintings used in storytelling, often depicting episodes from epics like Ramayana and Mahabharata

Features: Bold outlines, bright natural colors, and folk style

Paithani Sarees


Origin: Paithan town (Aurangabad district)

Material: Pure silk with zari (gold/silver thread)

Designs: Peacocks, lotuses, and geometrical motifs; vibrant colors

Kolhapuri Chappals


Origin: Kolhapur

Material: Leather

Features: Handcrafted, durable footwear, often decorated with braids and punched designs

Lavani


Lavani (from the word 'lavanya', meaning beauty) is a traditional folk dance and music style that combines powerful rhythm and bold, sensuous expression. It is performed by women wearing bright sarees, usually in a theatrical setting, and is deeply rooted in Maharashtrian rural and working-class culture.
